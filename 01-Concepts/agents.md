### 1. Key Themes

**Multi-Agent Architecture is the New Default**
All sources converge on the shift from single general-purpose AI to specialized agents working in concert. Whether it's coding, marketing, operations, or research—decomposition into specialized agents beats monolithic approaches.

**The Bottleneck Has Shifted to Knowledge Management**
Having capable agents isn't the constraint anymore. The hard problem is feeding them the right context, maintaining shared state, and enabling persistent memory across sessions. Raw compute is cheap; coherent knowledge architecture is scarce.

**Solo Scale is Now Feasible**
AI agents compress headcount. What required teams now requires orchestration. The billion-dollar one-person company isn't hype—it's a plausible endgame when specialized agents handle execution and the founder focuses on direction and relationships.

**Audience Before Infrastructure**
Multiple sources emphasize building audience and distribution *before* automating fulfillment. Agents are wasted on products no one wants. Validation comes first; automation amplifies what works.
